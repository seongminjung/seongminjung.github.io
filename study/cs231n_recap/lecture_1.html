CS231n Recap

Lecture 1 | Introduction to Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition

Posted on March 26, 2024

CS231n

CS231n is a course on Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition. It is taught by Stanford University. The course is available on YouTube.

Topics

The first lecture is an introduction to Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for visual recognition.

The rest of the lecture covers the following topics:

  • Lecture 2 | Image Classification
  • Lecture 3 | Loss Functions and Optimization
  • Lecture 4 | Introduction to Neural Networks
  • Lecture 5 | Convolutional Neural Networks
  • Lecture 6 | Training Neural Networks I
  • Lecture 7 | Training Neural Networks II
  • Lecture 8 | Deep Learning Software
  • Lecture 9 | CNN Architectures
  • Lecture 10 | Recurrent Neural Networks
  • Lecture 11 | Detection and Segmentation
  • Lecture 12 | Visualizing and Understanding
  • Lecture 13 | Generative Models
  • Lecture 14 | Deep Reinforcement Learning

Assignments

There are three assignments in the course:

  1. Image Classification, kNN, SVM, Softmax, Neural Network
  2. Fully-Connected Nets, Batch Normalization, Dropout, Convolutional Nets, Network Visualization
  3. Image Captioning with Vanilla RNNs, LSTMs, Transformers, Generative Adversarial Networks

I will post my solutions to the assignments in the future. Stay tuned for more updates on the CS231n Recap series!
